Listojo: What Has To Be True In The Next 12 Months

Prepared as a strategic execution requirements document

Executive view: Over the next 12 months, Listojo must prove that it can create qualified demand, convert a meaningful share of that demand, and measure revenue attribution with confidence.

1. Product Focus Must Be Clear

  • The team must define the primary wedge clearly.
  • The highest-probability wedge is curated rental and property discovery with optional agent assistance.
  • Non-core categories must remain secondary until the core workflow is proven.

2. Guided Search Must Become a Real Lead Engine

  • Guided search must capture structured intent, not just browsing clicks.
  • User preferences should feed matching, ranking, and agent workflows directly.
  • The handoff from self-serve to assisted path must be explicit, measurable, and useful.

3. Lead Routing Must Be Operationally Reliable

  • There must be clear rules for assignment, ownership, status, and follow-up.
  • Agent dashboards should show context-rich leads, not shallow contact submissions.
  • Response times and lead outcomes need visibility.

4. Supply Quality Must Improve

  • Listings need freshness, trust indicators, and cleaner relevance.
  • MLS ingestion, if added, must be governed carefully with source clarity and compliance discipline.
  • The platform should emphasize fewer, better listings over noisy volume.

5. Revenue Attribution Must Be Defensible

  • Referral-triggering events should be clearly defined.
  • Payout logic needs an audit trail.
  • The business must be able to show why revenue is attributable to platform influence.

6. The Product Must Be Instrumented

  • Track search starts, guided search completion, shortlist engagement, contact events, agent handoffs, and downstream outcomes.
  • Know where intent drops off and where conversion improves.
  • Measure cohort quality, not just traffic volume.

7. The Platform Must Stay Technically Disciplined

  • Preserve a modular monolith rather than letting business logic sprawl.
  • Separate domains such as search, leads, routing, listings, revenue, and operations.
  • Increase test coverage around business-critical flows.
  • Improve security, auditability, and deployment discipline before scale stress arrives.

8. Commercial Proof Must Exist

  • At least one segment should show repeatable value to both users and agents.
  • Agents should see better quality or better context than they get elsewhere.
  • Users should clearly experience better outcomes than raw marketplace search.

9. Bottom Line

If, within 12 months, Listojo can prove focused demand capture, better matching, disciplined lead routing, trusted attribution, and repeatable commercial outcomes, then the business will have earned the right to scale.
